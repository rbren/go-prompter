Barack Hussein Obama II (born August 4, 1961) is an American politician who served as the 44th president of the United States from 2009 to 2017. A member of the Democratic Party, he is the first African American to be elected to the presidency. He previously served as a United States senator from Illinois from 2005 to 2008 and as an Illinois state senator from 1997 to 2004.

Born in Honolulu, Hawaii, Obama graduated from Columbia University and Harvard Law School. After working as a community organizer in Chicago, he was elected to the Illinois Senate in 1996. In 2004, he was elected to the United States Senate, where he gained national recognition for his work on healthcare, immigration, and climate change. In 2008, he was elected president, defeating Republican nominee John McCain.

During his first term, Obama signed the Affordable Care Act into law, which expanded health insurance coverage to millions of Americans. He also oversaw the end of the Iraq War and the authorization of the raid that killed Osama bin Laden. In 2012, he was re-elected, defeating Republican nominee Mitt Romney. During his second term, he signed the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act, which was intended to prevent another financial crisis. He also promoted renewable energy and negotiated the Iran nuclear deal.

Obama left office with high approval ratings. After his presidency, he published a memoir, A Promised Land, and continued to work on social and political issues through the Obama Foundation. He is married to former First Lady Michelle Obama and they have two daughters, Malia and Sasha.

Barack Obama is an American politician and lawyer who served as the 44th President of the United States from 2009 to 2017. He was the first African American to hold the office. Key points about Barack Obama:

1. Born on August 4, 1961, in Honolulu, Hawaii.

2. Graduated from Columbia University (1983) and Harvard Law School (1991).

3. Worked as a civil rights attorney and taught constitutional law at the University of Chicago Law School.

4. Served in the Illinois State Senate from 1997 to 2004.

5. Elected as a U.S. Senator from Illinois in 2004.

6. Won the Democratic Party's presidential nomination in 2008 and defeated Republican nominee John McCain in the general election.

7. Served two terms as President, focusing on issues such as healthcare reform (Affordable Care Act), economic recovery, and climate change.

8. Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2009 for his efforts to strengthen international diplomacy and cooperation.

9. After his presidency, he has been involved in various charitable causes, public speaking, and writing, including his bestselling memoir "A Promised Land" (2020).

10. Married to Michelle Obama, with whom he has two daughters, Malia and Sasha.

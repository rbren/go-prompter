Barack Obama is an American politician and attorney who served as the 44th President of the United States from January 20, 2009, to January 20, 2017. Born on August 4, 1961, in Honolulu, Hawaii, Obama is a member of the Democratic Party. He was the first African American to be elected to the presidency. Prior to his presidency, Obama served as a U.S. Senator from Illinois from 2005 to 2008 and as a state senator in Illinois from 1997 to 2004.

Obama's presidency was marked by several significant events and legislative accomplishments. In domestic policy, he signed into law the Affordable Care Act (commonly known as Obamacare), which aimed to improve access to health insurance for Americans. He also signed the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act to regulate the financial sector in the aftermath of the 2008 financial crisis. In foreign policy, Obama's administration focused on winding down the Iraq War, ordering the military operation that killed Osama bin Laden, and negotiating the Iran nuclear deal.

After serving two terms, Obama was succeeded by Republican Donald Trump. Post-presidency, Obama has remained active in various political and social causes, public speaking, and has written a memoir titled "A Promised Land," which was published in November 2020. He is married to Michelle Obama, and they have two daughters, Malia and Sasha.
